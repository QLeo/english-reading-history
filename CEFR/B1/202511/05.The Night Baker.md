# The Night Baker

James had always been afraid of the dark.
Even as an adult, he felt uncomfortable when the sun went down.
This fear made his life difficult because he worked as a baker.
Bakers usually start working very early in the morning, often at 3 or 4 AM.
For James, walking to the bakery in the dark was a daily challenge.
Every morning, he would leave his apartment feeling nervous.
The empty streets and dark shadows made his heart beat faster.
He tried different ways to feel less afraid.
He listened to music through his headphones.
He called his sister and talked to her while walking.
Sometimes he even ran to the bakery to spend less time in the dark.
One winter morning, something changed.
James was walking to work when he saw an old woman sitting on a bench.
She looked cold and tired.
Without thinking, he stopped and asked if she needed help.
The woman explained that she was waiting for the first bus to the hospital.
Her husband was sick, and she wanted to be there early.
James sat with her and they talked for twenty minutes.
She told him stories about her life and her family.
When the bus finally came, she thanked him warmly.
James realized something important that morning.
While he was helping the old woman, he had completely forgotten about his fear.
He had been so focused on another person that the darkness didn't matter anymore.
From that day on, James started looking at his morning walks differently.
Instead of thinking about his fear, he began noticing other people.
He saw the street cleaner who worked hard to keep the city beautiful.
He noticed the newspaper delivery person riding a bicycle in all kinds of weather.
He even started saying good morning to the security guard at the office building.
These small interactions made him feel connected to his community.
The darkness was still there, but it felt less frightening.
James learned that sometimes the best way to overcome a fear is to stop focusing on it.
When we pay attention to others and try to help them, our own problems seem smaller.
Now, James actually enjoys his early morning walks.
He still arrives at the bakery at 4 AM, but the journey feels different.
The dark streets that once scared him have become a peaceful part of his day.

---

## 질문 (Questions)

1. What was James's problem, and why did it make his job difficult?
2. What happened when James met the old woman on the bench?
3. How did James's attitude toward his morning walks change?

## 숙지하면 좋은 문법 요소들

1. **Past Perfect (과거완료)**: "had always been afraid"
   - 과거의 특정 시점 이전부터 계속된 상태 표현

2. **Modal verbs (조동사)**: "would leave", "could", "didn't matter"
   - 습관, 가능성, 중요성 표현

3. **Past Continuous (과거 진행형)**: "was walking", "was waiting"
   - 과거의 진행 중인 행동 묘사

4. **Gerunds (동명사)**: "walking to the bakery", "helping the old woman"
   - 동사를 명사처럼 사용

5. **Causative 'make' (사역동사)**: "made his life difficult", "made his heart beat faster"
   - 무언가가 어떤 결과를 일으키게 함

6. **Comparative structures (비교 구조)**: "less afraid", "less frightening", "smaller"
   - 비교급 표현

7. **Time expressions (시간 표현)**: "Even as an adult", "From that day on", "Now"
   - 시간의 흐름과 변화 표현

8. **Infinitive of purpose (목적의 부정사)**: "to feel less afraid", "to spend less time", "to help them"
   - 목적을 나타내는 표현

9. **Present Perfect (현재완료)**: "have become"
   - 과거부터 현재까지의 변화 표현
